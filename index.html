    I am a postdoctoral researcher at Sabancı University, working in the MIDST laboratory with Canan Atilgan and Ali Rana Atilgan. My current focus is on understanding the mutational effects of proteins and explaining the molecular basis of deep mutational scanning (DMS) experiments through structural biology. To achieve this, I employ various computational tools, including molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, free energy perturbation (FEP) simulations, and protein structure prediction tools based on deep and machine learning.

    I use MD simulations to investigate the general dynamic features of proteins and FEP simulations to calculate relative binding energies, allowing me to corroborate computational findings with experimental results. I also utilize AlphaFold2 (AF2) for structure prediction; because AF2 leverages multiple sequence alignments (MSAs), different conformations can be predicted by using varying MSAs.

    In analyzing the produced conformations, I rely on measures such as hydrogen bonds, solvent-accessible surface area (SASA), relative solvent accessibility (RSA), and distances. Examining probability density functions and variances helps reveal subtle conformational changes.

    Additionally, I use residue networks to scrutinize protein structures. Applying graph theory and metrics like betweenness centrality (BC) and communities (via a BC-based Girvan-Newman algorithm), I aim to explain conformational differences by identifying which residues belong to the same community.

    If you are interested in learning more, check out my online course on Molecular Dynamics Simulations of Small Molecules.
